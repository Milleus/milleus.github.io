title: "Why technology is strange"
date: 2018-11-19
---

I think one of the worries in the past was opportunity. Depending on whether you're born in a rich or poor family, the opportunities you're presented with are vastly different. The rich are able to provide their kids with better services, better tools, access to information, and send them to better schools.

Today, technology has shrink that opportunity gap as information is freely available on the Internet and digital tools are getting cheaper.

But with it comes a new, strange phenomenon where almost every kid is now hooked onto the iPhone, iPad or digital television. I understand why - it's amazing how fast one of these devices can get a kid to sit still and have their dinner - but I question if we should really let our younger generation have access to these. I'm not sure what do they learn from the videos that they watch (what the hell does baby shark teach?), and I'm concern with whether we're getting them addicted to the Internet. Are we training the next generation of digital zombies? Can the next generation focus on tasks at hand? Will they end up becoming terrible people, given how terrible people can be behind anonymity?

Technology is strange, or perhaps it's us, humans, that are strange. We like to solve problems but we also created more problems with it - fix a bug and introduce 100 more bugs.
