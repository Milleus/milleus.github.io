Recently, I've been feeling rather overwhelmed by work. I feel like there's too many things to do in too little time. I guess the main problem is that it's difficult to find time to really sit down and focus on your task at hand; people asking questions, random discussions, meetings, interviews and so on. This comic pretty much sums up how I feel..

I think I speak for my colleagues too, most of us are preoccupied with something else other than development work. Not saying that it's a bad thing, in fact, I think it's a good thing because it gives us more ways to contribute to our organisation, to make a bigger impact.

Anyway, now that we're "stuck" in this situation where meetings and discussions are unavoidable, what can we do to be more productive? One of my colleagues mentioned something called the Pomodoro technique and I thought I'd give it a try.

### What is Pomodoro Technique?
This technique is basically a way to break down your work into 25 minute intervals (typically), with 3-5 minutes break in between. These 25 minute intervals are called pomodoros and 4 pomodoros form a set. After each set, you take a 15-30 minutes break. Basically you'll be working something like this.

1. Pomodoro  - 25min
- _Breaktime - 5min_
2. Pomodoro  - 25min
- _Breaktime - 5min_
3. Pomodoro  - 25min
- _Breaktime - 5min_
4. Pomodoro  - 25min
- _Breaktime - 15min_

What if someone needs you to do something while you're in your pomodoro? Well.. you're suppose to tell them to f*ck off in a nice way and that you'll get back to them later, possibly allocating a pomodoro interval for them. And if you manage to finish the task faster, then the rest of the time is meant for "overlearning"; practicing newly acquired skills beyong the point of mastery. Apparently there's some scientific study behind all this, more at [wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pomodoro_Technique){:target="_blank"}.

By the way, [Toggl app](https://www.toggl.com/){:target="_blank"} has an in-built pomodoro timer, pretty nifty.

### Thoughts on Pomodoro Technique
After trying it out for about a week, I can feel that it does help, but not much as I hoped. When it comes to discussions and stuff, yes, having a pomodoro does help keep conversations on topic, but when it comes to development work, a 25 minute pomodoro feels way too short. In fact, reading about it online, many developers also face the same issue and many suggest increasing the time interval to 45 minutes or an hour.

Overall, I feel that I'm more aware of my time spent, I feel like I can prioritize my tasks better, the 5 minutes break gives me time to "re-align" my thoughts, and in a weird way, it does make me want to work more efficiently especially when a menial task takes too long. Perhaps, the improvements might not feel like much, but this is definitely better than before.
