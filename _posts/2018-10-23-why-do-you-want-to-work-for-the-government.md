"Why do you want to work for the government?". This is a very common question that I get every time I meet an old friend or someone new, and we're talking about what we're up to. There are a lot of assumptions made to the government culture, my life decisions of wanting an "iron rice bowl", how things are working in the government, and so on. Sometimes, I let it slide and let people have their own perceptions, but frankly there's a lot more as to why, and I thought I'd explain my thought processes to this.

Why I joined the Government Technology Agency of Singapore (GovTech) Hive:

1. Culture
2. Value
3. The Bigger Picture (something Singaporeans should think about)

### Culture

First of all, Hive is not the typical 9 to 5 job stuck in a cubicle. We embrace an open culture, team structure is rather flat, we can feedback to our managers at any point of time with no repercussions, flexible working hours, work from home concept, leave early if you have to, and so on. We have fun events, we have table tennis tables, foosball tables, all kinds of stuff. We're like Facebook and Google, but government.

As long as you get your shit done, you'll have time to do your own stuff. I like this because it allows me to work on bigger things, to relax, to innovate, to learn, to explore, to experiment. I'm not saying that it's 100% perfect as every culture has it's own problems (perhaps I'll talk about this in another post), but it is definitely different and provides a lot of opportunities to better ourselves.

### Value

Harness info-communications technologies to make a difference to everyday lives of people in Singapore. That's pretty much what we stand for, and it's something I believe in and I feel I can contribute to. Over time, I've seen that what we're doing is different, we believe in user-centric designs, we have room to be bold to experiment, explore and innovate, so that we can come up with better ideas and make better decisions. Some projects which we've worked on are Parking.sg, HDB Resale Portal, Moments of Life, OneService, and many more. Getting positive feedback from people that I've met who have used either of these products makes me proud of what we're doing.

### The Bigger Picture

These are just my perception on things, but I do feel that it is important for us, Singaporeans, to think about. As we all know, Singapore is reliant on our sea ports and tourism, and there's been a lot of news on what our neighbouring countries are doing to take away our competitive advantage. China has been investing in a Malaysian port to compete with Singapore. China has also been investing heavily in a new silk road, which includes land and maritime routes, again these routes may be economically cheaper and faster. There's also investment in Thailand's Kra Isthmus Canal or known as Kra Canal which basically cut sea journey between Indian Ocean and South China Sea, essentially bypassing our ports. If we think about these things happening around us, I think it is only a matter of time before we lose our competitive advantage.

I assume this is why our government is pushing for us to be the next Silicon Valley, to push for innovation and advancements in technology. Because countries such as Israel and Estonia are kind of similar to us, and yet they are able to thrive through technology. The good thing is Singapore is small, so we're able to move faster than the giants out there, and I think GovTech plays a big role in this push for technology advancement. If we want to advance faster, I think it's important that the government leads by example, and I feel that this is what GovTech is doing.

Perhaps my views are flawed or biased, and maybe these are not cause for concern, but I do feel that as humans we tend to think too small, be comfortable and complain about the small things in life. I would like to challenge you to think bigger and consider the "what ifs", for our future, for Singapore's future.
